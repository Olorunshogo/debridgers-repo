## Vision &amp; Mission: Stephanie!!!

**Vision**

A Nigeria where every family and business pays the true price of food with no exploutation, no information gap and no middlemen tax.

Debridgers envisions a future where the agricultural value chain in Nigeria is transparent, efficient, and fair.
A country where farmers earn what their produce is worth, and consumers pay only what it actually costs to get food from farm to table.

---

**Mission**

To eliminate exploitative middlemen from Nigeria&apos;s food supply chain by connecting farmers directly to consumers and wholesalers through technology, trusted agents and transparent pricing.

Breaking it down, that translates to:

- Sourcing directly from farmers at fair prices
- Using a network of verified field agents to bridge the last mile
- Delivering to homes and shops at the same price you&apos;d pay at the market. No markup, no hidden fees
- Starting in Kaduna, scaling across Nigeria

---

**Where Debridgers Should Be in 5 Years**

By **2031**, Debridgers should be the dominant farm-to-door platform across Northern Nigeria.

Specifically:

- Operating in at least 10 major cities (Kaduna, Kano, Abuja, Zaria, Jos, Sokoto, etc.)
- A network of 200+ verified field agents earning sustainable commissions
- Partnerships with farmer cooperatives and state agricultural boards
- A mobile app with 100,000+ active users (consumers and wholesalers)
- A wholesale arm: small businesses, restaurants, and market traders ordering in bulk at negotiated rates
- Transparent weekly pricing published publicly, becoming a reference point for fair market rates in the region
- Paystack-powered seamless payments with buy-now-pay-later options for bulk buyers

**Where Debridgers Should Be in 10 Years**

By **2036**, Debridgers should be the infrastructure layer of Nigeria&apos;s food economy.

Specifically:

- Pan-Nigeria presence: all 36 states, with regional hubs managing logistics
- A farmer-facing platform where smallholder farmers list produce, get advance payments, and track demand forecasts
- A data business: selling anonymized supply/demand insights to government, NGOs, and food security organizations
- Cold chain logistics infrastructure (refrigerated storage and transport) owned or partnered
- Expansion into West Africa: Ghana, Senegal, Côte d'Ivoire. Where the same middleman exploitation problem exists
- A financial services layer: micro-loans for farmers, crop insurance, and savings products for agents
- Recognized by the Nigerian government and international bodies (FAO, World Bank) as a model for agricultural value chain reform

## Agrolinking

**The Problem with This Partnership as-is**

The entire value proposition to consumers is "market prices, zero stress."
If Agrolinking selling to us at ₦1,400/bag - the same price a customer would pay at the market, then we have zero margin to cover:

- Delivery costs
- Agent commissions (your backend already calculates 5% commission on sales)
- Platform operations
- Any free delivery promotions you're running

We&apos;d be losing money on every order. That&apos;s not a partnership in my books, that&apos;s a liability.

---

**The Real Question: Are They Actually a Partner?**

A true supply partner for Debridgers should be selling to you below market price because, we'll be offering them:

- Guaranteed bulk volume
- Consistent, predictable demand
- Faster payment than retail
- No market stall overhead for them

If they can&apos;t price below market, they&apos;re just another middleman. Which is exactly what we&apos;re trying to eliminate.

---

**Questions to Ask in the Meeting**

**On Pricing**

- What is your farm-gate cost per bag? (You need to know their actual margin)
- What price do you sell to regular market traders at?
- What volume would unlock a lower price for us — 50 bags? 100? 500?
- Can we agree on a tiered pricing structure as our orders scale?

**On Their Supply Chain**

- Are you sourcing directly from farmers, or do you also have middlemen above you?
- How many farmers are in your network?
- WhatApos;s your supply consistency: can you guarantee X bags per week regardless of season?
- What happens during off-season or shortage periods: do prices spike?

**On the Relationship**

- Are you offering us the same price you offer walk-in buyers at the market?
- What makes this an exclusive or preferential partnership vs. just a regular transaction?
- Are you open to a formal supply agreement with locked pricing for 3–6 months?

**On Quality**

- What&apos;s your quality control process before bags leave your facility?
- What&apos;s your policy if we receive substandard produce?
- Do you have grading standards (Grade A vs. B)?

---

**Our Negotiation Position**

The thing is, we&apos;re not just a one-time buyer. We&pos;re building a recurring demand channel for you.
As we grow in Kaduna and expand to other cities, your volume grows with us.
In exchange for that guaranteed, growing demand, we need a price that lets us operate - ideally 15–25% below current market rate.

Agrolinking should understand that that we need margin to exist.
If they treat us like a retail customer, they&apos;re not aligned with our mission.
